在中国，应试教育是已经保持多年的教育方式，高考也被视为跳跃阶层，走向光明未来的唯一途径。在艺术人才的选拔方式上，也由应试教育衍生出了艺术应试考试-联考，这种教育方式极大的损害了艺术学者的创造力，为中国艺术领域带来冲击。作为亲身经历过美术应试教育与考试的我，对这种考试制度有着深深的不满，因此我想基于此现状去说一些什么。该项目以表演作为载体，去模拟一场美术考试，通过使用一系列的标准化工具，去绘画出一个“标准答案”定义之下的苹果。以这种形式的表演来讽刺当下美术应试教育功利主义导向之下的刻板化教学，只注重结果，而放弃艺术学习中最重要的过程。

Under China’s harsh censorship, the queer community is forced to be invisible. The Chinese government does not allow content related to homosexuality to be normalized in front of the general public. As a group with high prestige and positive images in Chinese society, athletes are regarded as a part of the national image in China. Thus, they are not allowed to have a negative influence on the public. Queer athletes are in a dilemma. Though they want to create a positive image for their country and their career, they are unable to achieve true self-identity, which is a very sad situation.Queer cues and queer messages are satirically combined with the celebrating activities for athletes. This is a message to the queer community in sports arenas that they are not alone in their battles. They are backed up by the whole community. This aims to separate them from the national and political attributes, try to give queer athletes a sense of belonging and enhance their self-identity.
